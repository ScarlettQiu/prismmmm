Key Change in Round 10 — PyMC Saturation Prior
Root cause identified: pymc-marketing's default saturation_beta ~ HalfNormal(1.5) allowed any channel to explain up to ~150% of max KPI at saturation. For sparse channels (google_shopping, many zero-spend weeks) and high-spend channels (google_search), this created prior-dominated ROI estimates unconstrained by data. Tightening to HalfNormal(0.5) limits each channel to ~40% of max KPI, consistent with Ridge and BayesianRidge estimates.
Effect: google_search PyMC ROI dropped from 100.6× (R9) to 48.6× (R10) — now within 1.2× of Ridge (40.1×), giving CV 28.1%. meta_instagram PyMC ROI dropped from 3.27× to 2.92×, reducing CV from 41.4% to 35.6%. meta_facebook unchanged (CV 3.2%, all three models 1.48–1.60×).
Cross-Model Agreement: Round 9 vs Round 10
google_search crossed below CV 50% for the first time — CV dropped from 60.7% (R9) to 28.1% (R10). All three models now agree on direction and order of magnitude. This is the first Google channel to show reliable cross-model signal in 10 rounds.

Key Takeaways
1. meta_facebook remains the most defensible finding. CV 3.2% across Ridge (1.48×), PyMC (1.52×), and BayesianRidge (1.60×) — confirmed positive ROI for 10 consecutive rounds. Recommendation to maintain or increase budget is defensible at CFO level.
2. meta_instagram is strengthening. CV fell from 41.4% (R9) to 35.6% (R10). Direction consistent across all three models. One more round near this level would cross the 20% high-agreement threshold.
3. google_search achieved cross-model signal for the first time. CV 28.1% (was 60.7% in R9). All three models agree on direction and rough magnitude (23–49×). High ROI reflects demand capture rather than demand creation. Confirmed at medium signal — not yet sufficient for budget recommendation.
4. PyMC divergences remain an issue. 27 NUTS divergences after tuning indicate the tighter prior creates difficult posterior geometry. Test MAPE elevated at 42.8%. Round 11 should try target_accept=0.95 to reduce divergences and assess if test MAPE recovers.
5. Google Shopping/Display/Video/PMax still unreliable. Sign disagreements across models persist. Structural collinearity among Google channels (all 4 correlated with each other) prevents reliable individual attribution. These channels should not inform budget decisions until a model with explicit collinearity handling (e.g., ridge-constrained MMM) is applied.
