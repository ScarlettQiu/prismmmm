Key Insights
Round 4 → 5: results are stable. Despite fixing two real bugs (PyMC wrong axis, LightweightMMM scaling), the NNLS contributions and ROI rankings barely moved. This validates Round 4's findings — they weren't artefacts of coding errors.
Multi-model code review caught real issues. GPT-4o and Claude reviewed 6 pipeline scripts in parallel. Claude flagged a PyMC axis error (mean(axis=1) vs mean(axis=0)) that would produce wrong posterior predictions. The bug fix is now in production.
PyMC MAPE slightly higher in R5 (14.1% vs 13.1%). The axis fix changes how the posterior predictive mean is computed — previously it was inadvertently averaging differently, which may have coincidentally produced a lower MAPE. The corrected version is statistically sound even if the metric is slightly higher.
Google Shopping remains the highest-ROI channel (41×) across R4 and R5. However, it is also one of the highest-disagreement channels (CV > 100%) — this extreme ROI likely reflects very low spend rather than true efficiency. Treat as a signal to test scale, not a budget allocation target.
