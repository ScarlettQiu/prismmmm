Key Insights Across R8 → R9 → R10
meta_facebook is the most durable finding in the project. It was confirmed in R7 (CV 6.5%), degraded in R8 only because of a BayesianRidge attribution bug, and has been re-confirmed at CV <5% for two consecutive rounds. Mean ROI 1.53× across Ridge, PyMC, and BayesianRidge is defensible for CFO-level budget decisions.
R10 is a prior calibration milestone. Tightening saturation_beta ~ HalfNormal(0.5) pulled PyMC's google_search estimate from 100.6× (R9) to 48.6× — within 1.2× of Ridge. This is the first time all three models agree on a Google channel direction and order of magnitude.
google_search CV history tells a story of model calibration, not data change. R8: 23.3% (artefact — BayesianRidge was broken). R9: 60.7% (BayesianRidge fixed, PyMC over-estimation exposed). R10: 28.1% (PyMC prior tightened). The underlying data didn't change between rounds — the models did.
27 NUTS divergences in R10 PyMC. The tighter prior improved CV% but created difficult posterior geometry. Round 11 should try target_accept=0.95 or reparameterising the saturation function. If divergences drop below 10, test MAPE should recover toward the R7–R8 range.
