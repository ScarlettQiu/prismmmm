Round 10 · Changes

What changed in Round 10

One targeted prior change to address PyMC prior dominance — and it moved three channels meaningfully.

Key Fix
Tightened PyMC saturation_beta prior
PyMC's Hill saturation prior was HalfNormal(σ=1.5) — too wide, allowing unrealistic saturation shapes that inflated channel ROIs. Changed to HalfNormal(σ=0.5), reducing prior dominance over sparse channel data.
Before: HalfNormal(1.5) → After: HalfNormal(0.5)
Effect on meta_instagram
CV 41.4% → 35.6% — promoted to medium signal
Tighter saturation prior reduced PyMC's outlier estimate for instagram, bringing it into closer agreement with Ridge and BayesianRidge. Cross-model variance is still above 30% but improving steadily.
R9: 41.4% ⚠️ WEAK → R10: 35.6% MEDIUM
Effect on google_search
CV 61.0% → 28.1% — first Google below 30%
google_search is the first Google channel to achieve cross-model agreement below 30% CV. Ridge 40.1×, PyMC 48.6×, BayesianRidge 23.3× — still high absolute ROI (efficiency spend at low total volume) but directional consensus is now present.
R9: 61.0% ❌ → R10: 28.1% MEDIUM
Still uncertain
Google Shopping, PMax, Display, Video
google_shopping CV 119.7% with one model at 230× (PyMC) vs 9.6× (BayesianRidge) — prior dominance still present for sparse-spend channels. google_pmax/display/video retain sign disagreements across models. No action warranted on these channels.

Round 10 · Recommendations

Actions after 10 rounds

01
Maintain or increase meta_facebook budget
meta_facebook is the only channel confirmed across all 10 rounds. CV 3.2% — Ridge 1.48×, PyMC 1.52×, BayesianRidge 1.60× all agree tightly. Every dollar spent on meta_facebook returns approximately $1.53 in attributable first-purchase revenue. This is robust enough for budget planning.
02
Watch meta_instagram — medium signal, improving
CV improving from 50.8% (R9) to 35.6% (R10). Mean ROI 1.96× is higher than meta_facebook but confidence is too wide for reallocation. A further 2–3 rounds targeting the PyMC saturation prior for instagram may bring CV below 20%. Do not reallocate yet — watch the next round.
03
google_search: emerging signal, do not act on absolute ROI
37.3× mean ROI reflects efficiency at low spend volume, not a scalable return. CV 28.1% is the first credible Google signal but directional only. The high absolute value is typical of low-spend channels where any attribution amplifies ROI. If google_search budget is a fraction of Meta, a modest test increase with controlled holdout would be appropriate.
04
Do not act on Google Shopping, PMax, Display, or Video
Sign disagreements across models indicate these channels are not yet identifiable from the data. google_shopping CV 119.7% with PyMC at 230× is prior-dominated. No budget decision should be made on these channels until sign disagreements are resolved — which may require additional spend data or longer time series.
Bottom line after Round 10: meta_facebook is confirmed — maintain budget. meta_instagram is trending toward confirmation — watch. google_search has its first real signal — small test budget increase acceptable. All other Google channels remain uncertain. The tighter PyMC saturation prior is working; continue tightening for instagram in Round 11.
