Why do the models differ?

Ridge attributes 100% to baseline because L2 regularisation shrinks all channel coefficients toward zero when predictors are correlated on only 10 training observations. NNLS, without the L2 penalty, allocates 24.6% to media — within a plausible MMM range for a growing retailer.

August 2015 Anomaly
GMV = INR 419,196 in a dataset where every other month exceeds INR 176M. Almost certainly a data entry error — missing zeros or wrong month. It inflates train MAPE to 310–877% and distorts all model diagnostics.
Fix this first
02
📏
10 Observations, 8 Channels
10 training rows cannot support 8 channel predictors simultaneously. Zero ROI on 6 channels means unmeasurable — not ineffective. Weekly data for 12+ months (52+ obs) is the minimum needed.
Structural constraint
03
🔗
Collinearity
Affiliates, Sponsorship, and Online.marketing all peak in the same months (Sep/Nov 2015, Mar 2016). Monthly data cannot separate their contributions. Weekly or daily data with more natural spend variation is needed.
Requires more data
07 — Recommendations

Three actions, in priority order

01
Protect Affiliates budget
Three rounds, two model types, consistent ROI of 0.75–0.90. This is the strongest signal the current dataset can produce. Do not reduce Affiliates spend pending a better-powered analysis.
02
Fix the August 2015 data point
INR 419,196 is almost certainly a data entry error. Correcting it may unlock measurable signal for the 6 zero-ROI channels. Check source systems against the raw transaction log — this is a one-day investigation.
03
Collect weekly data for 18+ months before re-running
Monthly gives 10 training rows — statistically underpowered. Weekly gives 78+ observations over 18 months, enough to support all 8 channels simultaneously with proper uncertainty bounds.
Bottom line: Affiliates shows consistent positive ROI across three independent rounds and two model types. For all other channels, do not make budget decisions based on this analysis.
